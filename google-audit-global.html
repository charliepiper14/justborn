The J-Born UK and Global account operates across four distinct markets and carries the same structural DNA as the EU account strong headline ROAS inflated by brand campaigns, a 4.4x acquisition ceiling on PMax, and an algorithmic channel lock that confines over 99% of PMax spend to Google Search. At current spend levels the account is broadly profitable. The problem, just as in the EU, is that it cannot scale.

The 30-day picture is more urgent than the 90-day. Middle East PMax ROAS has collapsed from 4.42x to 2.99x below the 3.8x breakeven and is spending nearly £1,000 per month at a loss. Three further UK campaigns (BIBS, Meal/MAM, Demand Gen) are at or below breakeven. Weekly CPA has risen 35% within the last 30 days alone. The account is not just stalling it is actively retreating.

Set against this are two genuine positives. The ALL Brand | Search campaign has improved its impression share from under 10% to 59.27% in the last 30 days a structural fix that is already working. And two campaigns, UK PMAX Bottle and UK Feed | UP, are trending consistently upward across all three time windows.

Core Problem

Why Scaling Keeps Failing Six Compounding Failures

1. PMax Channel Lock 99.2% in Search

Across every time window, over 99% of PMax budget flows to Google Search. YouTube receives 0.4% (£33 in 30 days). Gmail 0.3%. Discover 0.03%. GDN 0.01%. The algorithm has learned to behave like an expensive search campaign and abandoned the full top-of-funnel opportunity PMax is designed to deliver. The channel lock is worsening over time, not improving.

2. CSS Programme Issue 9,103 Products Restricted

The product diagnostics screen confirms 7,826 not-eligible products and 1,277 eligible-but-limited products. The dominant issue is "Unsupported country for CSS programme" affecting 524+ product records. The 1,277 limited products are accounting for £4,815 in ad spend across all campaign types with constrained reach. Separately, 34+ products are out of stock and still triggering impressions. Missing attributes (size, colour, age group) compound the feed quality problem.

3. Brand Impression Share Historically Near Zero

For most of the 90-day window, ALL Brand | Search was losing over 84% of brand searches to rank with impression share under 15%. Brand searches convert at 14–32% CVR with CPAs under £1. Every missed brand search is an almost-guaranteed sale given away. The fix has worked in the last 30 days (IS now 59%) but this should have been caught months ago.

4. CPA Deteriorating Week-Over-Week

Within the 30-day window, weekly CPA has risen from £4.96 to £6.70 a 35% increase in a single month. This is the signature of an algorithm running out of fresh, high-intent audience. The bidding system is being forced to bid deeper into lower-quality inventory to maintain volume, pushing up costs without proportional return. Without new audience signals (better asset groups, expanded channels, retargeting) this trajectory continues.

5. IS Lost Rank 80%+ Across the Board

Search lost IS to rank sits at 77.89% overall (30-day) and has been consistently above 72% across all three windows. The account is present in fewer than 23% of available relevant searches. This is not a budget problem it is a Quality Score and bidding infrastructure problem. The same structural ceiling visible in the EU account appears here too.

6. ME PMAX Spending Below Breakeven

The Middle East PMax campaign is spending approximately £30/day at 2.99x ROAS below the 3.8x breakeven. Every day it runs without intervention, it burns capital that could fund UK scaling. It has no organic ceiling preventing a further deterioration to 2x or lower given the Arabic conversion data.

CSS Programme What This Means

The "Unsupported country for CSS programme" issue is distinct from the EU account's data quality suspension. It means J-Born is participating in a Comparison Shopping Service (CSS) partner programme, but some products are being flagged because the CSS partner is not approved to serve ads in certain territories likely the Middle East and Israeli markets. This restricts those products to "Eligible (limited)" status, reducing auction participation and inflating CPCs.

1,277 Eligible (limited) products received £4,815 in Shopping spend in the last 30 days 53% of all Shopping budget but with artificially constrained auction reach. Resolving the CSS country coverage issue would immediately expand inventory access and reduce the CPC inflation caused by limited competition.

Top products by spend are all marked Eligible (limited). The JBØRN Personalised Stainless Steel Kids Water Bottle variants alone consumed £572–£466 each in the 30-day window. These are the account's highest-volume products running at reduced capacity due to a CSS configuration issue that can be fixed.

The fix path. Either ensure the CSS partner covers all relevant territories, switch affected products to standard Google Shopping (non-CSS) for those markets, or use separate Merchant Center feeds per territory. This is an administrative fix, not a creative or strategic one.

Key Search Intelligence Findings

The Bjørn effect is a strategic asset. People searching for BabyBjörn water bottles are landing on J-Born and converting at 30% CVR at a £1.18 CPA. This happens because the brand names are visually similar and the products overlap. Rather than treating this as accidental, it should be engineered: J-Born's personalised stainless steel bottles are a direct premium alternative and deserve explicit positioning against the Björn category.

Personalisation intent terms are the highest converters. "Personalised dummy clip" (17.3%), "personalised dummies" (16.8%), "personalised water bottle kids" (14.9%) every term that includes the personalisation intent outperforms its generic equivalent. This is the brand's moat. Every ad, every landing page, every Shopping image should lead with the personalisation message.

Search terms show zero conversions across the board in the overview view not because brand terms don't convert, but because conversion attribution in the search terms overlay is broken for the same reasons identified in the EU audit. The campaign-level data and individual search term analysis confirms these terms do convert at high rates. The fix is at the tracking configuration level.

Why This Happens and What It Costs

When PMax has insufficient creative assets, audience signals, or conversion data, the algorithm defaults to the path of least resistance: search. It has learned from existing purchase data which searches convert, so it concentrates spend there. Without YouTube-ready video assets, high-quality display images with text overlays, and strong audience signal lists, it will continue to behave like a search campaign.

The cost of this behaviour is not just the missed YouTube impressions it is the audience exhaustion cycle. By targeting the same search queries repeatedly, the algorithm gradually works through the highest-intent pool and is forced to bid on lower-intent queries at higher CPCs. This is the mechanism behind the 35% weekly CPA increase observed in the 30-day data.

The same issue exists in the EU account. Both accounts are running PMax with the algorithm trapped in search. This is a systemic infrastructure problem across the entire J-Born advertising operation not a single-account anomaly. Resolving it in one account while leaving the other untouched means solving half the problem.

What PMax Needs to Break Out of Search

Asset Requirement

Video Assets for YouTube

15-second and 30-second product demonstrations showing the personalisation process. "Name engraved in 24 hours" as a hook. J-Born's in-house creative team can produce these. Without video, YouTube inventory remains inaccessible.

Asset Requirement

Display-Ready Image Overlays

Square and landscape images with embedded text (product name, price, personalisation CTA). Current feed images work for Shopping but not for Display creative requirements.

Signal Requirement

Audience Signal Lists

Customer lists (past purchasers, email subscribers), site visitor segments, and in-market audiences (baby products, gifting). These give the algorithm permission to find new audiences across all channels.

Signal Requirement

Conversion Data Enrichment

Fix any tracking gaps so the algorithm receives clean purchase signals. Enhanced conversions, server-side tracking, and GA4 integration all contribute to a richer signal that lets PMax expand its targeting model.

Scaling Roadmap

Breaking Through the Wall Phase by Phase

The roadmap is sequenced deliberately. Each phase unlocks the next. Pushing budget before fixing infrastructure produces the same result that has played out for the last 18 months.

Phase 1 Weeks 1 to 2 · Stop the Bleeding

Emergency Actions

Pause ME PMAX | TP immediately (2.99x ROAS, below breakeven, £30/day cash burn). Reduce IS PMAX budgets by 30% while language segmentation is prepared. Fix the CSS programme country configuration to convert 1,277 limited products to fully eligible. Remove out-of-stock products from feed. Reactivate USA Brand | Shop and ME Brand | Shop (both paused missing free brand revenue). Split IS/ME/US Brand | Shop into three separate campaigns with territory-appropriate bids. Target: reclaim £900/month from ME waste and fix feed.

Phase 2 Weeks 3 to 6 · Fix the Foundation

Infrastructure Repair

Rebuild IS PMAX with Hebrew-language asset groups and Hebrew audience signals separated from Arabic inventory. Verify and repair conversion tracking across all campaigns ensure Add to Cart and Begin Checkout are firing correctly. Add missing product attributes (size, colour, age group) to affected products. Build dedicated customer list audience signals and upload to all PMax campaigns. Review BIBS and Meal/MAM campaigns for creative refresh both are in multi-month ROAS decline. Target: BIBS back above 4.5x, Meal/MAM back above 4.0x.

Phase 3 Weeks 7 to 12 · Unlock the Channels

PMax Expansion

Introduce video creative assets (15s and 30s) to UK PMax campaigns. With fixed audience signals and video assets in place, Google's algorithm can begin distributing spend across YouTube, Discover, and Gmail. Begin testing Demand Gen as a proper awareness driver with video currently it is running at 2.96x without adequate creative. Scale UK PMAX Bottle (5.26x, consistently improving) by 20% week-on-week. Scale UK Feed UP (5.13x) similarly. Target: PMax search share drops from 99% to 85%, releasing 14% into YouTube/Discovery.

Phase 4 Months 4 to 9 · Scale What Works

Market Expansion and Revenue Growth

With UK PMax stabilised above 5x and channel diversified, increase UK daily spend 15% every two weeks. Re-test Middle East (UAE only initially) with Arabic-language creative built specifically for personalisation intent. Re-evaluate Israel at higher Hebrew-optimised budgets. Use new clothing line launch as a PMax creative refresh moment new product categories reset the algorithm's learning and create fresh audience expansion opportunities. Target: £1,000+ daily UK spend at 5.5x+ ROAS.
