J-Born has built an impressive business from a standing start: nearly one million euros in annual revenue, a strong product line, fast fulfilment, and a creative team producing quality content. The EU Google Ads account shows healthy efficiency at current spend levels, with account-level ROAS exceeding the 5.5x target on paper. But that headline number is misleading.

The 5.83x account ROAS is heavily inflated by brand search campaigns capturing existing demand at 92.77x ROAS. Strip those out and the true acquisition engine, Performance Max, delivers 4.25x ROAS. That number only holds because spend has been pulled back to safe levels. When the account has attempted to scale beyond roughly 1,000 euros per day, ROAS collapses. This pattern has repeated for over 18 months and defeated previous agencies.

The root cause is not competition, creative quality, or market saturation. Auction insights show virtually no competitors in J-Born's paid search space. The scaling wall is caused by a combination of infrastructure failures: an 11.2-second mobile load time destroying conversions on 86% of traffic, broken conversion tracking sending corrupted signals to Google's bidding algorithm, a product feed with 60% of items marked ineligible, and search impression share at just 21.6% with over 80% lost to rank. Every one of these problems is fixable. Most can be resolved within 30 to 60 days.

04 / 15
Core Problem

Why Scaling Keeps Failing

The scaling wall is not caused by one issue. It is the compound effect of six infrastructure failures that amplify each other when budgets increase. At low spend levels, the system can tolerate these weaknesses. At higher spend, they compound and ROAS collapses.

1. Mobile Speed Crisis

Mobile PageSpeed: 29/100. LCP: 11.2 seconds. The target for LCP is under 2.5 seconds. J-Born is 4.5x over that threshold. With 86% of ad traffic on mobile, every visitor waits nearly 12 seconds for the main content to load. Industry data shows roughly 7% conversion rate loss per second after the 2-second mark. At these speeds, an estimated 50 to 70% of potential conversions are being destroyed before visitors ever see the product.

2. Conversion Tracking Broken

Begin Checkout and Add to Cart events are misconfigured. Screenshots of the conversion goal settings confirm these critical events are not firing properly. Meanwhile, irrelevant goals like Phone Call Lead, Contact, and Get Directions are active across campaigns. Google is partially optimising PMax toward local business actions instead of purely toward purchases.

3. Impression Share at 21.6%

Over 80% of available search impressions are lost to rank. The EU Brand Search campaign, at 92.77x ROAS, only captures 30% of available brand searches with 71% lost to rank. J-Born is missing over 70% of people actively searching for the brand name. When they do appear, they rank at the top 96.4% of the time. The problem is not competitiveness. It is visibility.

4. PMax Channel Concentration

PMax spending 99.6% on Google Search. YouTube gets 0.1%, Display gets 0.1%, Discover gets 0.1%, Gmail gets 0.2%. Performance Max is designed to find customers across all Google surfaces. This account is functioning as an expensive Search campaign, missing the entire top-of-funnel discovery opportunity that PMax is built to deliver.

5. Product Feed Failures

60% of products marked "Not eligible" in Merchant Center. The feed shows a "Suspended account for data quality issues" flag and widespread "Out of stock" classifications. Fewer eligible products means less inventory for PMax and Shopping to work with, constraining the algorithm's ability to find new customers through product-led discovery.

6. CPC Inflation While Volume Contracts

Search CPC up 67% (€0.09 to €0.15). PMax CPC up 23% (€0.31 to €0.38). The account is paying more per click while getting fewer impressions. This is the hallmark of audience exhaustion: the same users are being shown ads repeatedly at progressively higher costs.

What This Means for Scaling

The majority of customers need more than one interaction before purchasing. The 2.8-day average conversion window means retargeting within the first 3 days is critical. However, the current account structure lacks a dedicated retargeting strategy for Google. Site visitors, cart abandoners, and engaged users are not being systematically re-engaged.

Brand Search does double duty. It generates 92 click-and-view assists worth €4,573 in the 30-day window, on top of its direct conversions. Under data-driven attribution, Brand Search receives 26% fewer conversions credited than under last-click, while PMax campaigns gain credit. This means PMax true performance is slightly better than campaign-level reports suggest.

Cross-Device Patterns

7% of conversions involve cross-device paths (20 conversions in the 30-day window). The primary pattern is mobile browsing followed by desktop purchase. This is further evidence of the mobile speed problem: users discover products on mobile through PMax ads, but the slow mobile site experience pushes them to complete the purchase later on desktop where the site loads faster (desktop LCP is 1.4 seconds vs mobile at 11.2 seconds).

Model Comparison

When switching from last-click to data-driven attribution, the account shows 13.09% more total conversions (177 to 200.17). Brand Search drops 25.66% in credited conversions while PMax campaigns gain. The algorithm recognises that PMax is doing more upstream discovery work than last-click reports show.

Root Causes

The main culprits flagged by PageSpeed are render-blocking resources, unoptimised images, excessive JavaScript, and layout shift from dynamically loaded elements. The personalisation preview feature, review widgets, and Shopify app scripts are the likely contributors.

This is the single highest-impact fix available. At current traffic volumes with 86% mobile, the difference between an 11-second and a 3-second load time could double the PMax conversion rate from 2.79% to over 5%. That alone would push PMax ROAS from 4.25x toward the 5.5x target without changing a single ad or budget.

Desktop is a different story. At 39/100 with 1.4-second LCP, the desktop experience is dramatically faster. This explains why cross-device conversion patterns exist: users who browse on mobile but complete on desktop are escaping the mobile speed problem.

Conversion Tracking

The Algorithm Is Getting Bad Data

Misconfigured Events

Screenshots from the Google Ads conversion settings reveal multiple critical issues with the tracking setup. Begin Checkout and Add to Cart events are misconfigured, which explains why all checkout data in the reports shows 0.00 across the board.

Page View tracking is also misconfigured. These are the three most important mid-funnel signals that Google uses to optimise bidding in Performance Max campaigns. Without accurate data on who is adding to cart and beginning checkout, the algorithm cannot efficiently identify and target high-intent shoppers.

Irrelevant conversion goals are active. Phone Call Lead, Contact, and Get Directions goals are enabled across the account. These are local business actions that have no relevance to an e-commerce operation. Google is partially optimising PMax toward generating phone calls and map directions alongside purchases.

Goal Configuration Issues

Most campaigns optimise for a mixed goal set: [BEGIN_CHECKOUT/WEBSITE, DOWNLOAD/APP, PURCHASE/WEBSITE]. The DOWNLOAD/APP goal should not exist in an e-commerce campaign. Some campaigns correctly optimise for [PURCHASE/WEBSITE] only, creating an inconsistent setup.

Combined impact. Broken checkout tracking plus irrelevant goals plus inconsistent goal configuration means Google's Smart Bidding is operating on fundamentally flawed data. This is why performance deteriorates at scale: the algorithm's errors compound as more budget flows through the system.

The fix is straightforward. Remove Phone Call Lead, Contact, and Get Directions goals. Remove the DOWNLOAD/APP goal. Fix Begin Checkout and Add to Cart event configuration. Standardise all campaigns to optimise for PURCHASE/WEBSITE only. Verify tracking fires correctly using Google Tag Assistant. This can be completed within the first week.

CRO Analysis

Site Experience Review

Homepage Issues

The hero section does not communicate the personalisation USP above the fold for cold traffic. Visitors who have never heard of J-Born need to immediately understand what makes the brand different: personalised baby products with same-day engraving and next-day delivery.

Product Detail Pages

Review counts are low. A premium gift set shows only a handful of reviews, providing insufficient social proof. Trust badges are present but buried below the fold. The personalisation options are well-executed and front-and-centre, which is a genuine strength.

AOV optimisation is undercooked. Product pages show only a few cross-sell suggestions. There is no bundle builder, no visible free shipping threshold, and no urgency triggers. With a small average order value being a stated challenge, every PDP visit needs to work harder to increase basket size.

What Works Well

The personalisation preview feature is excellent and gives the site a genuine competitive advantage. Multiple payment options are available. The checkout process appears clean once users reach it. Trust badges are present throughout.

Key Recommendations

Homepage hero rewrite. Replace the generic hero with a headline that leads with personalisation. Communicate engraved names on premium baby products with same-day turnaround immediately.

Social proof sprint. Implement a post-delivery review generation programme. Feature aggregate ratings prominently on PDPs. Consider importing reviews from marketplace channels.

Free shipping threshold. Add a visible progress bar showing proximity to free shipping. This is one of the most reliable AOV levers in e-commerce.

Scaling Roadmap

Breaking Through the Wall

The scaling wall will not break by simply increasing budgets. Each phase addresses specific infrastructure failures that must be resolved before spend can be increased sustainably.

Phase 1: Fix the Foundation (Weeks 1 to 4)

Stop the Bleeding

Remove irrelevant conversion goals (Phone Call, Contact, Get Directions, Download/App). Fix Begin Checkout and Add to Cart event tracking. Standardise all campaigns to optimise for Purchase only. Resolve Merchant Center data quality suspension. Fix product feed to move 60% ineligible products to eligible status. Begin mobile page speed optimisation (image compression, lazy loading, script audit). Target: restore tracking integrity and feed health.

Phase 2: Unlock Channels (Weeks 5 to 8)

Expand Reach Beyond Search

With tracking fixed and the feed restored, begin testing PMax channel expansion into YouTube, Display, and Discover. Improve asset group quality across the 215 Poor-rated groups. Build dedicated retargeting campaigns for site visitors and cart abandoners. Increase Brand Search impression share from 30% toward 70% by adjusting bid strategy. Target: PMax ROAS stable at 4.5x+ on 30% higher daily spend.

Phase 3: Scale (Weeks 9 to 16)

Push Budget Into What Works

Increase daily spend by 15 to 20% per week on campaigns exceeding 5.0x ROAS. Expand EU5 campaigns into additional markets beyond IT, DE, FR, NL, ES. Launch new product categories (clothing line) with dedicated PMax campaigns. Implement homepage and PDP CRO improvements. Scale to €800 to €1,200 per day. Target: account ROAS at 5.5x with monthly revenue of €150,000+.

Phase 4: Compound (Months 5 to 12)

Reach the Revenue Target

Maintain 4 to 6 week creative refresh cycle. Scale to €1,500+ per day across EU markets. Expand into new markets that previously underperformed (now with fixed infrastructure). Optimise for lifetime value through post-purchase email flows and repeat purchase campaigns. Target: €240,000 to €250,000 monthly revenue from EU Google Ads.
